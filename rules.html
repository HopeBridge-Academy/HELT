Examination Conduct
Rules & Regulations
  • 1
    Non-Linear Navigation Prohibited. This exam follows a strict linear progression modelled on TOEFL iBT. Once you advance to the next question or section, you cannot return. Answer every item carefully before proceeding.
  • 2
    Strict Automatic Time Limits. Each section has a countdown timer visible at all times. When time expires, your current responses are automatically submitted and the exam proceeds to the next section. Unanswered questions receive zero marks.
  • 3
    Screen & Microphone Monitoring. Your screen and microphone are recorded throughout the entire examination via your browser. This recording is used exclusively for academic integrity verification by authorised examiners.
  • 4
    Tab Switching is Detected and Recorded. Switching to another tab, window, or application is logged. After the first switch, a warning is issued. Repeated switching may result in automatic disqualification.
  • 5
    No External Assistance Permitted. The use of dictionaries, translation software, AI tools, search engines, or assistance from any other person is strictly prohibited. All work must be your own.
  • 6
    Copy, Paste & Right-Click Disabled. Clipboard operations and right-click menus are disabled throughout the examination for security purposes.
  • 7
    Scores Not Disclosed During Exam. In accordance with standard testing practice, no section scores — including automatically graded scores — are displayed during the examination. All scores are released together with the official result.
  • 8
    Listening Audio: Maximum Two Plays. The listening passage may be played a maximum of two (2) times before you proceed to the questions. After two plays, the audio is locked.
  • 9
    Speaking: Two Mandatory Tasks. The Speaking section consists of two separate tasks. Each task has a 60-second preparation period followed by a maximum 120-second recording. Both must be completed.
  • 10
    Stable Connection Required. Ensure a stable internet connection before starting. Connection loss does not entitle a candidate to a re-test. Save your work where possible.
  • 11
    Minimum Word Count. The Writing section requires a minimum word count. Responses below the minimum incur an automatic deduction on the Task Achievement criterion.
  • 12
    Payment Mandatory for Results. Examination results and official certificates are issued only after confirmed payment of USD $5 / AFN 300. Payment proof must be uploaded at the end of the examination.
Section Guidance
What to Expect in Each Section
✍️
Writing (30 min · 25 marks): You will respond to an academic prompt with a minimum 220-word essay. Assessment criteria: Task Achievement, Coherence & Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy. Your response is pre-scored by AI and then reviewed by a certified human examiner.
📖
Reading (25 min · 25 marks): Read one academic passage and answer 5 multiple-choice questions (5 marks each). Questions are displayed one at a time; you cannot return to previous questions. Graded automatically.
🎧
Listening (20 min · 25 marks): Listen to an academic podcast (text-to-speech). You may play the audio up to 2 times. Then answer 5 multiple-choice questions one at a time. You cannot return to previous questions. Graded automatically.
🎙️
Speaking (15 min · 25 marks): You will complete two speaking tasks. Each begins with a 60-second preparation phase, followed by up to 120 seconds of recorded speaking. Grant microphone permission when prompted. Your recordings are pre-scored by AI and reviewed by a human examiner.
📜 Results & Certificate

Upon examination completion and confirmed payment, your Writing and Speaking responses will be reviewed by a certified examiner within two (2) working days. Your official HopeBridge English Language Certificate — bearing your full name, father's name, ID number, exam date, total score, proficiency level, and CEFR band — will be emailed to your registered address.

🔐
All scores (including auto-graded sections) are withheld until the final result is ready, ensuring assessment integrity and preventing partial-result speculation.
